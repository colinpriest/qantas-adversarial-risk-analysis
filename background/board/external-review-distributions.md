### 1. The Prior: Base Rate for Board-Initiated Reviews

Even for a firm in crisis, the starting "prior" remains the structural tendency for board-initiated reviews to report progress.

* **Positive Findings:** ~88%.

* **Balanced/Neutral Findings:** ~7-10%.

* **Negative Findings:** ~3-5%.

### 2. Updating Based on Evidence (The September 2023 Context)

The evidence to update this prior includes: (1) a history of labor disputes and customer service meltdowns, (2) a 30% share price drop since March 2023, and (3) the ACCC "ghost flight" filing two weeks prior.

* **The Credibility Collapse (Updating "Positive"):** Signaling theory suggests that a "Positive" finding (a clean bill of health) in this environment would be viewed as "gaslighting" or a non-credible signal, likely triggering a further negative market reaction rather than a recovery.

* **The Legitimacy Defense (Updating "Balanced"):** Legitimacy theory posits that the board must admit to "mistakes" to regain the "social license to operate". A "Balanced/Neutral" finding (admitting "mistakes were made" but denying "deliberate wrongdoing") becomes the dominant strategic path to appease regulators and allow for the cancellation of executive bonuses.

* **The Material Failure Risk (Updating "Negative"):** While the ACCC action suggests material failure, board-commissioned reviews rarely conclude with findings of systemic criminal wrongdoing because doing so would create a "smoking gun" for the ongoing litigation.
